The Numbers workshop was a good practice of the Rule of Three before the quiz. The most tricky part was implementing the copy constructor and assignment operator correctly, ensuring that deep copies were made instead of just copying the pointers, which would have resulted in double-free errors. The load() function also required some consideration to avoid off by one errors when reading from the file.
The thing that I learned most from this exercise is the importance of distinguishing between the original object and the copy in determining whether to write back to the file on destruction.
